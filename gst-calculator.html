How GST Is Calculated

In Exclusive Mode: GST Amount = Taxable Amount × (GST Rate / 100). For example, if you have ₹2500 with 18% GST: GST = 2500 × 0.18 = ₹450. Total = ₹2500 + ₹450 = ₹2950.

In Inclusive Mode: The total amount you provide already includes GST. We extract the base by dividing by (1 + GST Rate / 100). For ₹2950 at 18% GST: Base = 2950 / 1.18 = ₹2500. GST = ₹450.

Why This Matters: Understanding GST calculation helps you verify invoices, check if discounts are applied before or after tax, and plan your business pricing correctly.

Frequently Asked Questions About GST

What items fall under each GST slab?

5% GST applies to essential items like food, medications, and basic goods. 12% covers mid-range products like clothing and electronics. 18% applies to luxury items, restaurants, and services. 28% is for "sin goods" like alcohol and tobacco. Check your product category in official GST guidelines.

How do I know if a price is inclusive or exclusive of GST?

Online retailers and marketplaces show prices inclusive of GST. B2B invoices often show exclusive prices with GST added separately. If unsure, ask the seller or check the invoice breakdown for a separate "GST" line item.

Can I claim GST as input credit in my business?

If you are registered as a GST taxpayer, you can claim GST paid on business purchases as Input Tax Credit (ITC) to reduce your output GST liability. This applies only to genuine business transactions, not personal purchases.

Is GST applicable on all services?

Most services are taxable under GST. However, certain services like transport of goods via railways, education in schools, and agricultural products have exemptions or lower rates. Consult GST council guidance for specific services.
