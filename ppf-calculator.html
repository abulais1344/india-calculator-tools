What Is PPF Calculator?

A PPF calculator is a long-term planning tool that estimates how your Public Provident Fund investment can grow over time. In India, PPF is widely used by salaried professionals, self-employed earners, and conservative investors who want stable compounding with tax efficiency. Instead of calculating year-by-year growth manually, this tool gives quick estimates for total investment, interest earned, and maturity corpus. It is especially useful when you are comparing retirement savings options, planning tax-efficient investments, or setting annual contribution targets. Because PPF usually runs over long periods, even small consistency in annual deposits can create a substantial maturity amount through compounding.

How Does It Work?

The calculator uses annual contribution, expected annual interest rate, and number of years. It then applies compounding logic to estimate future value. When contribution remains consistent, projection becomes simpler and helps with yearly budgeting. If rates change in future, you can re-run the estimate with revised assumptions. This makes the tool practical for planning and periodic reviews. For realistic use, many investors check projections once or twice a year to align with income changes.

Formula Explanation

A simplified annuity-style estimate can be represented as FV = C × [((1+r)^n - 1) / r], where C is annual contribution, r is annual rate, and n is years. If contribution is ₹1,50,000 for 15 years at 7.1%, projected maturity can be calculated quickly with this structure. Actual account behavior can vary slightly based on deposit timing and rule details, but this approach gives a strong planning baseline.

Example Calculation

Assume contribution of ₹1,50,000 per year for 15 years at 7.1%. Total invested is ₹22,50,000. Estimated maturity value is significantly higher due to compounding, and the difference represents interest earned. If the same contribution continues for extension years, the corpus can accelerate further because interest applies on an already larger balance.
