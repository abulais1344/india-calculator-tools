What Is RD Calculator?

An RD calculator helps estimate maturity amount for recurring deposits where you invest a fixed amount every month. In India, RD is popular among savers who prefer disciplined monthly contributions rather than lump-sum investing. It is commonly used by salaried employees, young earners, and households building short- to medium-term funds for goals such as education expenses, travel, emergency buffer, or festival spending. Instead of manually tracking monthly deposit growth, this calculator provides immediate visibility into expected maturity value, total invested amount, and estimated interest. This makes savings planning simpler and helps avoid guesswork.

How Does It Work?

For RD, money is deposited monthly and each installment earns interest for the remaining tenure. Earlier deposits earn for longer, later deposits earn for fewer months. The calculator applies monthly compounding approximation to estimate cumulative maturity. By changing monthly amount, tenure, and interest rate, you can compare scenarios quickly and choose a savings pace that matches your budget.

Formula Explanation

Unlike a single principal investment, RD maturity is a sum of many monthly contributions with compounding applied progressively. A practical simulation method updates balance each month as: New Balance = (Previous Balance + Monthly Deposit) × (1 + Monthly Rate). Repeating this across all months gives estimated maturity value. Interest earned is maturity minus total deposits.

Example Calculation

If monthly deposit is ₹5,000 for 60 months at 6.75% annual rate, total deposit is ₹3,00,000. Maturity estimate will be higher because each contribution earns interest for a different duration. This helps you understand whether your monthly deposit plan is sufficient for target amount.
