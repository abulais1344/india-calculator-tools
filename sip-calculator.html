How SIP Returns Are Calculated

Formula: FV = P × [((1+r)^n - 1) / r] × (1+r), where P is monthly investment, r is monthly return rate, and n is total months.

Example: ₹5,000/month at 12% annual return for 20 years = ₹17.31 lakhs invested + ₹39.15 lakhs returns = ₹56.46 lakhs final value.

Key Insight: The longer you invest, the more compound interest works in your favor. Doubling your investment period can triple your returns due to compounding.

Frequently Asked Questions About SIP

What is the minimum SIP amount I can invest?

Most mutual funds allow SIPs starting from ₹500 to ₹1,000 per month. Some premium funds may require higher minimums. Check your fund house's rules before investing.

Should I stop SIP during market crash?

No! Market crashes are ideal for SIP investors. Your fixed monthly amount buys more units at lower prices. Continuing SIP during downturns actually maximizes long-term returns (rupee cost averaging benefit).

What is the tax on SIP returns?

Tax depends on fund type and holding period. Equity funds held for 1+ year (long-term) enjoy 10% tax on gains over ₹1 lakh. Short-term gains are taxed as per your income slab. Use tax calculator for estimates.

What Is SIP Calculator?

A SIP calculator estimates how much wealth you can build by investing a fixed amount every month in mutual funds. SIP means Systematic Investment Plan, where you invest consistently instead of making one large lump-sum investment. This approach is popular in India because it supports disciplined investing and reduces market timing pressure. Rather than waiting for the "perfect" entry point, SIP spreads your purchases across market cycles. A calculator helps you set realistic targets by showing total invested amount, estimated returns, and final corpus. This is useful for goals such as child education, retirement planning, emergency corpus, or buying a home down payment. If you increase SIP amount gradually with salary growth, long-term corpus can improve significantly. How It Works

The SIP return formula uses compound growth with monthly contribution. Simplified formula: FV = P × [((1 + r)^n - 1) / r] × (1 + r), where P is monthly investment, r is monthly return rate, and n is number of months. For example, if annual return is 12%, monthly rate is 1% (0.01). If you invest for 20 years, n becomes 240 months. The formula then estimates future value assuming constant return and regular monthly contribution. In real markets, returns are not constant, so use this as a planning estimate rather than guaranteed outcome. Still, this gives a strong baseline for setting monthly contribution goals and understanding time-driven compounding benefits.

Tips and Use Cases

Use conservative return assumptions for planning, especially for critical goals. Many investors run two scenarios: optimistic and conservative. Consider increasing your SIP yearly (step-up SIP) to align with salary increments. For retirement goals, focus on longer tenure because compounding impact accelerates in later years. For short-term goals (under 3 years), equity SIP may not be suitable due to market volatility. If market corrections happen, continue SIP instead of stopping; this improves unit accumulation through rupee-cost averaging. Finally, combine SIP planning with tax and salary calculators on this website to create a full monthly budget and investment strategy.
