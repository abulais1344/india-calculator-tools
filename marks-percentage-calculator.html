Frequently Asked Questions

438 out of 500 percentage

438 ÷ 500 × 100 = 87.6%. This is an A grade (Excellent) result, well above the distinction threshold of 75% in most boards.

438 out of 500 in percentage

438 out of 500 in percentage is 87.6%. Use the same formula for any marks: (obtained marks ÷ total marks) × 100.

What is the percentage of 438 out of 500?

The percentage of 438 out of 500 is 87.6. Calculation: 438 ÷ 500 = 0.876, then multiply by 100.

How do I convert marks to percentage?

Divide your obtained marks by the total marks, then multiply by 100. Example: 345 out of 500 = (345/500) × 100 = 69%.

What percentage is required for distinction in Indian boards?

In Maharashtra (SSC/HSC), Distinction is 75% and above. In CBSE, 90%+ earns a 10 CGPA. Different boards have different cut-offs—always check your board's official grading scale.

Is percentage the same as percentile?

No. Percentage shows how many marks you got out of the total. Percentile shows how you performed compared to other students. For example, 80% means you got 80 out of 100 total marks. 80th percentile means you scored better than 80% of all students who appeared.

How is CGPA different from percentage?

CGPA (Cumulative Grade Point Average) is used by CBSE and many colleges. To convert CBSE CGPA to percentage: Percentage = CGPA × 9.5. So a CGPA of 9.0 = 85.5%.
