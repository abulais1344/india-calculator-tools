How In-Hand Salary Is Calculated

Step 1: Total Income = CTC + Bonus (if applicable).

Step 2: PF Deduction = (Salary excluding allowances) × PF% = typically 12% of basic.

Step 3: Taxable Income = Gross Salary - Deductions (varies by regime).

Step 4: Income Tax = Applied as per slabs (new or old regime).

Example: CTC ₹60L → Basic ₹4L/month → PF ₹48K → Tax deducted → Monthly take home ≈ ₹3.2-3.5L.

Frequently Asked Questions About Salary

What expenses are included in CTC?

CTC includes: Basic salary, HRA (House Rent Allowance), DA (Dearness Allowance), Medical Insurance, PF contribution, Gratuity provision, and other benefits. These are costs your employer bears for you.

Can I claim tax deductions in the new regime?

The new regime doesn't allow most deductions (HRA, LTA, 80C) but has lower tax rates. You get a standard deduction of ₹50,000. For most employees, new regime is beneficial, but compare for your specific situation.

When do I get access to my PF amount?

You can partially withdraw PF for education, marriage, or house purchase. Full withdrawal happens upon retirement or 2 months of unemployment. Interest earned on PF is also tax-free.

What Is Salary Calculator?

A salary calculator helps you estimate take-home income from annual CTC by accounting for key deductions such as PF and tax. In India, many offers are shared as CTC, but actual in-hand salary is lower due to statutory and tax components. This tool simplifies that conversion and helps employees plan monthly budgets more confidently. It is useful for job-switch comparisons, appraisal planning, and choosing old versus new tax regime. Instead of relying on rough assumptions, you can adjust bonus, PF percentage, and regime to see immediate impact on annual and monthly net income.

How It Works

The calculator starts with annual CTC and optional bonus, then estimates PF deduction based on configured percentage. Next, it calculates taxable income and applies tax slab logic according to selected regime. Total deductions are combined and net annual take-home is derived, followed by monthly in-hand estimate. While this is a planning model and exact payroll structure can vary by company, it gives a clear directional understanding of how gross package translates to usable cash flow.

Benefits

  • Converts CTC into practical monthly in-hand estimate.
  • Helps compare job offers on a net-pay basis.
  • Supports tax-regime decision planning.
  • Useful for budget planning before major expenses.
  • Improves salary negotiation with realistic numbers.

Tips and Use Cases

Always compare at least two scenarios: current package and expected revised package. If your deductions are high under old regime, test both tax regimes before final declaration. Include bonus assumptions carefully because performance-linked payouts may vary. Use this output with EMI calculator to ensure loan EMI remains affordable under your net monthly income, not just CTC headline value.

During offer negotiation, discuss fixed pay, variable pay, and employer contribution components separately. A slightly lower CTC with stronger fixed monthly payout can improve real budgeting stability.
