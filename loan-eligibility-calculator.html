How Loan Eligibility Is Calculated

Step 1: Maximum EMI allowed = Monthly Income × 50% = (₹50,000 × 0.5) = ₹25,000.

Step 2: Eligible EMI = Maximum EMI - Existing EMIs = ₹25,000 - ₹10,000 = ₹15,000.

Step 3: Using EMI formula, we calculate the loan amount: P = EMI × [((1+r)^n - 1) / (r × (1+r)^n)].

Example: With ₹15,000 eligible EMI at 8.5% for 20 years, maximum loan = ₹18.5 lakhs.

Frequently Asked Questions About Loan Eligibility

Why can't I get the full loan amount I'm eligible for?

Besides EMI capacity, banks consider your age, job stability, credit score, down payment amount, and property value. Eligibility here is based on EMI capacity alone. Your final loan amount may be lower.

How does existing debt affect my eligibility?

Every existing EMI (car loan, personal loan, credit card EMI) reduces your available EMI capacity. Clearing existing loans before applying for a new loan improves your eligibility significantly.

Is this calculator accurate for all banks?

This calculator uses the standard 50% income rule, which most banks follow. However, individual banks have different policies. Check with your specific bank for exact eligibility criteria.

What Is Loan Eligibility Calculator?

A loan eligibility calculator estimates how much loan amount you may qualify for based on your monthly income, existing EMIs, interest rate, and tenure. Before applying for a home loan, car loan, or personal loan, this tool gives a practical estimate of borrowing capacity so you can shortlist realistic options. Banks generally evaluate debt-to-income ratio and repayment ability. If your current EMI obligations are high, eligible loan amount reduces. This calculator simplifies that logic and helps avoid over-borrowing. It is useful for first-time borrowers, salaried professionals planning a property purchase, and families comparing affordability across different tenures.

How It Works

The calculator starts with a common affordability rule where around 50% of monthly income is treated as maximum safe EMI capacity. From that value, existing EMI obligations are deducted to get eligible EMI for a new loan. Then it reverse-applies the EMI formula to estimate principal amount supported by that EMI at the selected rate and tenure. In simple terms, if your eligible EMI is higher or tenure is longer, possible loan amount rises. If rate is higher or existing EMIs are heavy, possible loan amount drops. This gives a quick planning estimate before formal bank underwriting.

Benefits

  • Avoids unrealistic loan applications and rejection risk.
  • Shows impact of existing EMIs on borrowing power.
  • Helps choose better tenure for affordability.
  • Supports faster property or vehicle budgeting decisions.
  • Provides a practical starting point before bank discussion.

Tips and Use Cases

Before applying, try reducing high-interest debt to improve eligibility. Test multiple tenures to find the best balance between EMI affordability and total interest cost. Keep room for emergency expenses instead of using maximum eligible EMI. If two applicants are involved, compare individual and combined income scenarios for better planning. Use this tool with EMI and salary calculators to validate if the expected repayment is sustainable after tax and monthly essentials.

For home buying decisions, use this estimate as a pre-screening step and then validate with bank-specific eligibility checks such as credit score, FOIR policy, age limits, and property valuation rules.
