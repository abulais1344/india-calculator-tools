How Income Tax Is Calculated

New Regime: Apply tax slabs directly on income (no deductions). Tax brackets: 0-2.5L (0%), 2.5-5L (5%), 5-7.5L (10%), etc. Plus 3% Education Cess on total tax.

Old Regime: Deduct Section 80C (max ₹1.5L), 80D (max ₹25K), etc., from income first. Then apply slabs. Allows more deductions but higher tax rates.

Surcharge: Applied on tax if income exceeds certain limits (currently 1 Cr for individuals). Ranges from 10% to 37% based on income brackets.

Example: Income ₹80L → New Regime tax ≈ ₹10.5L. Old Regime with ₹1.5L deduction → Taxable ₹78.5L → Tax ≈ ₹10.3L.

Frequently Asked Questions About Income Tax

What does Section 80C, 80D mean?

These are deduction sections under the Income Tax Act. 80C allows deductions for life insurance, PPF, ELSS up to ₹1.5L. 80D covers health insurance premiums up to ₹25K (individuals) or ₹50K (families).

When should I choose old regime?

Old regime is better if you have significant deductions (₹2L+ from 80C, 80D, 80E, etc., combined). For most salaried employees with basic deductions, new regime is beneficial. Always compare both.

What is the standard deduction in new regime?

New regime provides a Standard Deduction of ₹50,000 (FY 2024-25 onwards) for salaried employees. This reduces your taxable income by ₹50K directly, simplifying tax calculation.

What Is Income Tax Calculator?

An income tax calculator estimates your annual tax liability based on taxable income, deductions, regime selection, surcharge, and cess. For Indian taxpayers, this is useful for advance planning before filing returns or selecting tax regime in payroll declarations. Instead of waiting for year-end surprises, you can project tax outgo and net income in advance. This helps with investment planning, monthly savings goals, and salary structuring decisions.

How It Works

The calculator applies slab-wise tax rates to taxable income and computes surcharge where income thresholds apply. Education cess is then added to the tax plus surcharge amount. Under old regime, eligible deductions like 80C/80D can reduce taxable income, while new regime generally offers lower slab rates with fewer deductions. The tool compares these effects and outputs total tax payable, effective tax rate, and net income estimate for practical decision-making.

Benefits

  • Gives quick estimate of annual tax payable.
  • Helps compare old versus new regime efficiently.
  • Shows effective tax rate for better planning.
  • Supports monthly post-tax budgeting decisions.
  • Useful for salaried and self-employed tax forecasting.

Tips and Use Cases

Run both regimes before finalizing investment declarations. If you already invest heavily under 80C and have medical insurance deductions, old regime may remain competitive. If deductions are limited, new regime may simplify planning and reduce compliance burden. Combine this output with salary calculator to see realistic post-tax cash flow for EMI and investment planning.

For quarterly planning, update this estimate whenever your bonus, business income, or deduction profile changes. Frequent recalculation helps avoid underpayment surprises near return filing time.

Use this number as a decision aid and confirm final tax with your latest Form 16, AIS, and applicable financial-year rules.
