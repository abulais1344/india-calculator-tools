Salary Needed for Home Loan in India: ₹30L, ₹50L, ₹1Cr Examples

Banks do not approve a loan only on property value. Your salary, existing EMIs, credit score, and FOIR decide eligibility. This guide gives practical salary benchmarks for common loan sizes.

1. The 50% Income-to-EMI (FOIR) Rule

Most banks use FOIR (Fixed Obligations to Income Ratio) to decide your repayment capacity.

FOIR = (All monthly EMIs / Monthly Income) × 100

  • Typical FOIR range: 40% to 55%
  • For safer planning, assume maximum EMI around 50% of monthly income
  • If you already pay EMIs, available capacity for home loan EMI is lower

Example: Monthly net income ₹1,00,000 and existing EMIs ₹15,000.

  • Max total EMI at 50% FOIR = ₹50,000
  • Available home-loan EMI = ₹50,000 - ₹15,000 = ₹35,000

4. What If You Already Have Existing EMIs?

Existing obligations reduce eligible home loan amount directly.

Example for ₹50L target loan:

  • Required home loan EMI: ~₹44,986
  • Existing car/personal loan EMI: ₹15,000
  • Total EMI capacity needed at 50% FOIR: ₹59,986
  • Required monthly income: ~₹1,20,000

Prepaying smaller high-interest loans before applying can significantly improve eligibility.

5. Co-Applicant Option: A Smart Way to Increase Eligibility

Adding an earning co-applicant (usually spouse, parent, or adult child) can increase sanctioned amount by combining incomes.

  • Combined income raises FOIR-based EMI capacity
  • Can improve loan amount and sometimes tenure flexibility
  • Both applicants' credit score and obligations are considered

Example: You earn ₹75,000 and spouse earns ₹55,000. Combined ₹1,30,000 income can support higher EMI than a single-applicant case.

6. 7 Ways to Improve Home Loan Eligibility

  1. Reduce existing EMIs before applying.
  2. Improve credit score (aim 750+).
  3. Increase down payment to reduce loan amount.
  4. Choose longer tenure for lower EMI (if suitable).
  5. Add co-applicant income.
  6. Show stable employment and regular bank credits.
  7. Avoid multiple loan inquiries in a short period.

7. Quick Planning Checklist Before You Apply

  • Compute affordable EMI using FOIR and your current obligations.
  • Test 1% higher interest scenario to check EMI stress.
  • Keep emergency fund of at least 6 months EMIs.
  • Compare offers from 2-3 lenders before finalizing.
  • Factor in registration, insurance, and maintenance costs.

Frequently Asked Questions

What salary is needed for a ₹50 lakh home loan?

For a ₹50 lakh loan over 20 years at around 9%, EMI is roughly ₹44,986. With 50% FOIR, monthly net income should be around ₹90,000 to ₹1,00,000 depending on existing obligations.

Can I get a home loan with 40,000 salary?

Possible loan size depends on tenure, interest rate, and existing EMIs. With ₹40,000 monthly income and 50% FOIR, affordable EMI may be around ₹20,000, which can support roughly ₹22-25 lakh loan at common rates.

What is FOIR in home loan eligibility?

FOIR stands for Fixed Obligations to Income Ratio. Banks usually allow total EMI obligations up to 40% to 55% of monthly income.

Does adding a co-applicant increase loan eligibility?

Yes. Adding an earning co-applicant increases combined income, which can raise eligible EMI and total loan amount, subject to credit profile and age criteria.

How can I increase home loan eligibility?

Reduce existing EMIs, improve credit score, increase down payment, choose longer tenure, add co-applicant income, and maintain stable job history to improve eligibility.
