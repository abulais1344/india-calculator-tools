How to Calculate Percentage of Marks – Formula, Examples & Grade Chart

The Formula

Percentage = (Marks Obtained ÷ Total Marks) × 100

That is all there is to it. Divide the marks you scored by the maximum possible marks, then multiply by 100 to get a percentage.

Step-by-Step Example

Question: What is 438 out of 500 as a percentage?

  1. Marks Obtained = 438
  2. Total Marks = 500
  3. Divide: 438 ÷ 500 = 0.876
  4. Multiply by 100: 0.876 × 100 = 87.6%

So 438 out of 500 is 87.6% — typically a Distinction in most Indian boards.

Marks Percentage on Different Total Marks

The formula works the same regardless of the total. Here are quick examples on different totals:

  • Out of 600 (SSC/HSC 6 subjects at 100 each): 480/600 = 80%
  • Out of 500 (5-subject board): 438/500 = 87.6%
  • Out of 400 (4-subject semester): 320/400 = 80%
  • Out of 300 (3-subject term): 240/300 = 80%
  • Out of 100 (single subject): 87/100 = 87%

Frequently Asked Questions

What is 438 out of 500 as a percentage?

438 out of 500 is 87.6%. Formula: (438 ÷ 500) × 100 = 87.6%.

What is 450 out of 500 percentage?

450 out of 500 is 90%. Formula: (450 ÷ 500) × 100 = 90%.

How do I calculate aggregate percentage?

For multiple subjects, add up all marks obtained across subjects, add up all total marks, then apply the formula: Total Obtained ÷ Total Maximum × 100. For example: scored 85, 90, 78, 92, 88 out of 100 each → Total = 433/500 = 86.6%.

Is 75% a Distinction?

In most Indian boards and universities, 75% and above is classified as Distinction. Some universities set the Distinction threshold at 70%.

How do I convert CGPA to percentage for CBSE?

CBSE recommends the formula: Percentage = CGPA × 9.5. For example, CGPA 9.0 × 9.5 = 85.5%.
