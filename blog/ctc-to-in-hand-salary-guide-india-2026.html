CTC to In-Hand Salary in India (2026 Guide)

Offer letter says one number, but your bank account receives another. This guide explains exactly how CTC converts into monthly in-hand salary in India.

Salary Planning Tax Basics Updated: May 2026 8 min read
Advertisement
Quick Answer: In-hand salary is usually lower than CTC because CTC includes employer-side benefits and mandatory deductions. Always evaluate salary offers using expected monthly take-home, not CTC alone.

What Is CTC and Why It Confuses Most People

CTC stands for Cost to Company, which is the total yearly amount an employer spends on your compensation package. It may include basic pay, HRA, special allowance, employer PF contribution, gratuity, bonuses, and other benefits.

Your in-hand salary is the actual monthly amount credited to your bank account after deductions such as employee PF, professional tax, and TDS. This is why two offers with similar CTC can result in different take-home pay.

Simple Formula: CTC to Monthly In-Hand

A practical way to estimate your in-hand salary is:

  • Start with annual CTC.
  • Remove employer-side components (employer PF, gratuity, etc.).
  • Subtract annual deductions (employee PF, professional tax, TDS).
  • Divide by 12 for estimated monthly in-hand salary.

Main Deductions That Reduce In-Hand Salary

1) Employee Provident Fund (PF)

PF is a retirement saving deduction, commonly 12% of basic salary. This amount is not lost; it goes to your provident fund account. But it does reduce monthly take-home cash.

2) Professional Tax

Professional tax is state-specific and usually small, but it still affects your monthly salary credit.

3) TDS on Salary

Employers deduct TDS based on projected annual taxable income. Comparing old and new tax regimes can significantly change your monthly in-hand amount.

How to Increase In-Hand Salary Without Breaking Rules

  • Compare old vs new tax regime every financial year.
  • Use eligible deductions and exemptions where available.
  • Negotiate a better salary structure, not just higher CTC.
  • Keep investments and declarations updated with HR on time.
  • Avoid waiting for year-end tax panic planning.

Offer Comparison Tip for Job Switchers

When comparing job offers, do not compare CTC alone. Ask for:

  • Detailed annual salary breakup
  • Monthly gross and monthly net projections
  • Fixed vs variable pay clarity
  • Joining bonus and retention clauses

This helps you avoid surprises after joining and makes your negotiation more data-driven.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between CTC and in-hand salary?

CTC is total annual package. In-hand salary is monthly salary after deductions like PF, professional tax, and TDS.

Why is my in-hand salary lower than expected?

Because CTC includes non-cash or deferred components and employer-side contributions. Tax and statutory deductions further reduce monthly credited salary.

How can I increase in-hand salary legally?

Optimize tax regime and salary structure, submit valid declarations, and plan deductions systematically throughout the year.
