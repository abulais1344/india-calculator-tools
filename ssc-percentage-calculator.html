Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate Maharashtra SSC percentage?

Add all 6 subject marks together and divide by 600, then multiply by 100. Example: 495 ÷ 600 × 100 = 82.5%.

What is passing marks in Maharashtra SSC?

You must score at least 35 out of 100 in each individual subject. Overall, aggregate 35% or more is required to pass.

Is SSC the same as Class 10 board exam?

Yes. In Maharashtra, SSC stands for Secondary School Certificate and is the official Class 10 board exam conducted by the Maharashtra State Board of Secondary & Higher Secondary Education (MSBSHSE).

How is SSC different from CBSE Class 10?

Maharashtra SSC uses a 600-mark system with 6 subjects graded A1–E. CBSE Class 10 uses a 9-point CGPA system out of 500 marks (Best of 5 subjects). The grading scales and calculation methods differ.
